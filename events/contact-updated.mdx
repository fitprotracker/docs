<Warning>
**`contact.updated` is not available for subscription in v1.1.**

This event was originally designed as a generic "any field changed" signal, but in practice a single contact often produces 3-4 near-identical events in the same second (a staff member saving phone, then notes, then group). That floods partner tools like Mailchimp and Zapier and is the wrong abstraction for most integrations.

v1.1 ships **four focused events** instead. v1.2 will add additional focused events to round out the contact lifecycle.
</Warning>

## Use these focused events instead

Pick the specific signal your integration actually cares about:

| Your use case | Subscribe to |
|---|---|
| "I want every new lead in my CRM" | [`contact.created`](/events/contact-created) |
| "I want to know when a lead converts to a member" | [`contact.status_changed`](/events/contact-status-changed) |
| "I want to know when someone is moved to a class group" | [`contact.sub_group_changed`](/events/contact-sub-group-changed) |
| "I want to remove customers who cancel from my marketing list" | [`contact.deleted`](/events/contact-deleted) |

## What's coming in v1.2

The events below are designed and on the schedule. They replace the catch-all `contact.updated` with discrete, partner-friendly signals — subscribe by the specific change you want to react to, not by every database write.

| Event | Fires when |
|---|---|
| `contact.identity_updated` | First name, last name, email, or phone is changed |
| `contact.address_updated` | City, state, postal code, or country is changed |
| `contact.note_added` | A note is added to the contact |
| `contact.tag_added` / `contact.tag_removed` | A tag is added to or removed from the contact |

If you have a v1.2 use case that none of these cover, [tell us](mailto:webhooks@fitprotracker.com) — we'd rather hear about it now while the design is still flexible.
